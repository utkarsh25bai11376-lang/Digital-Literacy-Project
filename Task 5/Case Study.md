# Case Study:UPI Fraud

# UPI Fraud Overview

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) scams rank among India's most prevalent cybercrimes, where fraudsters deceive users into sending money or disclosing bank details via bogus calls, texts, or links.

# How Scams Unfold

Fraudsters reach out via phone, SMS, or social media, impersonating bank staff, support agents, or trusted contacts. They urge victims to tap malicious links or scan fake QR codes, then coax them into sharing UPI PINs or approving payments. Funds transfer instantly to the scammer's account once the victim complies.

# Common Targets

Students, seniors, and those unfamiliar with digital payment safeguards face the highest risks, especially frequent app users unaware of security protocols.

# Impacts

Victims suffer immediate financial losses, emotional distress, eroded faith in digital banking, and potential data theft for additional crimes—often with little chance of fund recovery.

# Key Takeaways

Stay vigilant: Never share UPI PINs, avoid unsolicited links, and always confirm request legitimacy before transacting to safeguard against these threats.
